Get out of everyday life for a cosmic journey through the planets of our solar system!

Some like their holidays active, others like things slow. There is no shame in going on holiday, kicking back and doing absolutely nothing, yet so often in reality it doesn't quite work out like that. Arrive in a new destination, and most of us will instantly want to head out and explore every nook and cranny. We just can't help ourselves.

We have come up with a few locations that will help you a hand in resolving this conondrum. These destinations feature some of the most spectacular scenery seen anywhere in the world, but there is not much to do other than gawk at it in awe, and kick back...

1. Mercury

Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, where temperatures swing from hotter than a pizza oven during the day to colder than a freezer at night. It's like the ultimate test of thermal endurance - you'll get a tan and frostbite on the same trip!

2. Venus

Welcome to Venus, where the greenhouse effect is so strong that it makes Florida summers feel like a refreshing breeze. Pack your sunscreen and a snorkel because you're in for a steamy ride through sulfuric acid clouds!

3. Mars

Mars, the red planet, where Elon Musk dreams of building a civilization while the rest of us wonder if we'll ever find Matt Damon growing potatoes there. It's like a barren desert, but with a killer view of Earth.

4. Jupiter

Jupiter, the behemoth of the solar system, where the storms rage for centuries and the gravity will crush you like a soda can. Don't forget your umbrella, because it rains diamonds here!

5. Saturn

Saturn, the planet with the blingiest rings in town. It's like a cosmic hula-hoop contest, where the winner gets to show off their jewelry to the whole galaxy. Just watch out for those pesky ice chunks!

6. Uranus

Ah, Uranus, the butt of every astronomy joke. With its sideways rotation and icy atmosphere, it's like the rebellious teenager of the solar system, constantly flipping off its peers.

7. Neptune

Neptune, the icy giant at the edge of the solar system, where the winds blow faster than a Formula 1 race car and the atmosphere is colder than your ex's heart. Bring a sweater and hold on tight!

8. Pluto

Poor Pluto, kicked out of the planet club and demoted to a mere "dwarf planet". It's like the underdog of the solar system, forever overshadowed by its bigger siblings. But hey, size isn't everything, right?

Welcome aboard the Interstellar Express! Today, we're embarking on a cosmic adventure to our neighboring galaxies, where stars twinkle like disco balls and black holes are the tourist hotspots.

Andromeda

The closest galaxy to the Milky Way, about 2.5 million light-years away. It is also one of the largest and brightest galaxies in the Local Group.

Triangulum Galaxy

It is a spiral galaxy similar to the Milky Way, located about 3 million light-years away. It is one of the most distant galaxies visible to the naked eye in a dark sky.

Magellanic Clouds

These are two small irregular galaxies that are satellites of the Milky Way: the Large Magellanic Cloud and the Small Magellanic Cloud. They are visible from the southern hemisphere and are among the closest galaxies to the Milky Way, situated about 160,000 light-years away.

NGC 6822 Galaxy

Also known as Barnard's Galaxy, it is an irregular dwarf galaxy that is part of the Local Group, located about 1.6 million light-years away.

Sagittarius Galaxy

This is a dwarf spheroidal galaxy orbiting the Milky Way, situated about 70,000 light-years away.
